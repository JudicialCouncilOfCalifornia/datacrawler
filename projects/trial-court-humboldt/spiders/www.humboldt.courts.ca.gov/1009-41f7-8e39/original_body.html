      Jury Services

      Response to the COVID-19

      The court has implemented the following in response to the COVID-19. These changes have been reviewed and approved by the Humboldt County Public Health Doctor assigned to the court.

      a. Social distancing
      b. Face masks/face coverings are required for everyone who comes to the courthouse. Please bring and wear a face mask/face covering.
      c. Fewer jurors are being summoned.
      d. Bring your jury summons with you on the day you report.
      e. Bring your own bottled water, as a common water dispenser will not be available.
      f. Health Screening Questions will be asked of all prospective jurors.
      g. Jury trials have limited seating
      h. Social distancing is maintained throughout the entire jury trial process, i.e. assembly, empanelment and deliberation.
      i. Fewer trials are being held.

      Jury Duty

      JURY DUTY - An Honored Service

      The right to a trial by jury is a privilege that applies to both criminal and civil cases and is recognized as the foundation of the American court system, guaranteed by both the United States and California Constitutions.

      Jury trials cannot be held unless people like you are willing to perform their civic duty. Jurors are essential to the administration of justice. Check your jury status at the Jury Summons Website and click here for information on Court Etiquette and Security.

      Jury Summons Website

      SCAM ALERT:
      Jury Staff of the Superior Court of California, County of Humboldt will never ask past or prospective jurors for financial information, credit card numbers, bank account information, or personal information like Social Security Numbers. Do not provide this type of information to anyone claiming to be associated with the courts. For more information, click here.
