      Self-Help

      The Court’s self-help centers provide assistance in the areas of both family law and non-family (civil) law. These services are available to all self-represented parties, regardless of income. The self-help center staff can provide you with information on your legal rights and obligations; they cannot provide you with legal advice. You should contact a private attorney if you want personalized advice or strategy, to have a confidential conversation, or to be represented by an attorney in court. Please click on the links below for more information in the area in which you need help.

      Services are available through workshops and appointments. In order to provide better assistance and service, the Court’s self-help center no longer accepts walk-ins.
